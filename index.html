About Me

Researcher, Lecturer

I am Lecturer @ NIDA

Sangkae Punyasiri is Assistant Professor of the Graduate School of Tourism Management at National Institute of Development Administration. She received her B.S.(Accounting) from Bangkok University, B.S. (Computer Science) from Heidelberg University, M.B.A. (Organization Behavior) from Northrop University, USA., and D.B.A. in Management Science from University of South Australia, Australia. She teaches Financial and Investment Planning for Tourism and Hospitality Business, which is GSTM’s valuable course for Master of Management (Integrated Tourism and Hospitality Management).

    Previously,

    Sangkae served as Consultant for Department of Tourism of Thailand. She advises on the Thai Tourism Standard Model: Bicycle Service Standard, Street food Service Standard, Van Service Standard, Service and Safety Standard in Shopping Center for Tourism, and Tourism Destination Management Standard. Sangkae’s research in graduate school focused primarily on Women in Management and work-life balance. Her dissertation was entitled Work and Family Conflict of women managers in Hotel Industry. Her more recent publications focus on employee engagement and organizational citizenship behavior.
